🌿 Georgia Habitats Biodiversity Project

🎯 Your Task

You will be assigned one Georgia habitat. Your job is to research and create a Google Slides presentation showing what makes that ecosystem unique — including its location, abiotic and biotic features, and how organisms interact.

📌 Assigned Habitats

  • Coast
  • Mountains
  • Ocean
  • Piedmont
  • Swamp (Okefenokee)

Your teacher will assign your group’s habitat.

📋 What to Include (1 Slide Each)

  • Slide 1 – Title Slide: Your name, class period, habitat name, image
  • Slide 2 – Location: Map or picture + “My habitat is found in…”
  • Slide 3 – Abiotic Factors: At least 3 non-living features (soil, temperature, etc.)
  • Slide 4 – Flora and Fauna: 1 native plant, 2+ native animals with images
  • Slide 5 – Species Interactions: 3 interactions (competition, predation, symbiosis)
  • Slide 6 – Biodiversity Importance: Why should we protect this habitat?
  • Slide 7 – Sources: At least 2 research sources (links or titles)

🧠 Sentence Starters (Use in Your Slides)

  • “One abiotic factor in my habitat is _____ because _____.”
  • “A native plant is _____, and it survives by _____.”
  • “Two animals are _____ and _____. They live here because _____.”
  • “An example of competition is…”
  • “In this habitat, _____ eats _____.”
  • “A symbiotic relationship is between _____ and _____ because…”

🔍 Where to Research

  • Georgia Biodiversity Portal
  • UGA Habitats of Georgia
  • Georgia Wildlife Division

📤 How to Turn It In

  • Open the Google Slides template provided by your teacher
  • Edit each slide with your research, text, and images
  • Check for spelling and sentence completion
  • Share with your teacher or submit on Canvas

✅ Grading Rubric (15 Points Total)

This is how your project will be graded. Do your best to meet the expectations in each column!

Category Excellent (5 pts) Meets (3–4 pts) Needs Work (1–2 pts)
Research Accuracy Information is correct, clear, and includes Georgia-specific facts. Mostly accurate, some missing or unclear details. Incomplete, vague, or incorrect information.
Species Interactions 3 examples with correct science terms (competition, predation, symbiosis). 2 examples shown; missing some explanation or vocabulary. 1 or no interactions shown; terms not used correctly.
Visuals & Effort Includes labeled images, neat layout, and thoughtful design. Some visuals included; layout is mostly complete. Few or no visuals; work appears rushed or incomplete.
